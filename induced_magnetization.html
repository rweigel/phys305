1 Introduction

In Continuous Magnetic Dipole Distributions, the problem of finding the magnetic field due to an object that had a magnetization M ‚Äúfrozen in‚Äù was considered.

The approach was to find the bound currents that result from this magnetization. Then the magnetic field due to the bound currents was computed.

Consider an object that is unmagnetized (M=0), but is placed in an external magnetic field, Bext‚Äã. In response to this magnetic field, atomic‚Äìsized current loops will tend to align with the external magnetic field. This alignment creates a magnetic field, which called an induced magnetic field.

The total field is then

Btotal‚Äã=Bexternal‚Äã+Binduced‚Äã

By convention, we drop the ‚Äútotal‚Äù subscript and abbreviate ‚Äúexternal‚Äù as ‚Äúext‚Äù. Given that the induced field is can be computed from bound current densities, we can use Bb‚Äã in place of Binduced‚Äã to emphasize this point. In this notation, we have

B=Bext‚Äã+Bb‚Äã

It is important to note that Bb‚Äã depends on B.

Without additional information, it is not possible to compute B ‚Äì we need to know how much M is generated given B. Once we have M, we can compute the bound currents and then Bb‚Äã.

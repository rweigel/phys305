1 Introduction

In general, finding V V V for a boundary value problem requires the solution Poisson’s equation

which is a partial differential equation (PDE). However, for certain problems, V V V can be found without without solving a PDE. The method of solution is called The Method of Images.

The list of basic problems that can be solved with the method of images includes

  1. V V V for z > 0 z>0 z>0 when a point charge is at z = d z=d z=d and the x – y x–y x–y plane is grounded and conducting and d / R ≪ 1 d/R \ll 1 d/R≪1. The induced charge will be negative and largest near the origin.

  2. V V V inside/outside of a grounded and long conducting cylindrical shell when a uniform line of charge runs parallel to the centerline of the cylinder and is outside/inside of it.

  3. V V V inside/outside of a grounded conducting spherical shell when a point charge is outside/inside of the shell. The induced charge will be negative and largest near the q q q. The radius of the sphere is not constrained. The pattern of induced charge for q q q outside of the spherical shell is shown in the following figure.

Given that there are only a few problems that can be solved by this method, and that the problems are highly idealized, it would seem that this method is not generally useful. However, it turns out that the solutions to these basic problems can be used to solve more complex problems. (These more complex problems are typically covered in a graduate–level E&M course.)

The method of images uses the fact that solutions to Poisson’s equation are unique. As a result, if you can guess V V V that satisfies the boundary conditions and Poisson’s equation, you have found the solution. The “images” in Method of Images comes from the fact that the guess of the solution is based on finding V V V for an alternative problem than the one given. In the case of 1., the alternative problem uses an “image” of the given charge that is below the x – y x–y x–y plane. (If the plane was a mirror, the position of the charge in the mirror would be behind the plane.)

If the V V V for the different problem satisfies the boundary conditions for the given problem, it is the solution.
